Assignment 1

Introduction

For this assignment I have used the "Average Computer Prices in Dollars by Computer Brand" dataset from Kaggle. Each chart was carefully chosen to highlight different aspects of the data. Below are the visualizations along with explanations of why each was selected and what they communicate.

1. Column Chart

I chose a column chart to represent the average price of computers by brand because it is straightforward and effective for comparing categorical data. The height of each column directly indicates the average price, making it easy to see differences between brands.

This chart communicates the average pricing of different computer brands. For example, brands with taller columns have higher average prices, indicating they might be positioned as premium brands, while brands with shorter columns could be targeting budget-conscious consumers.

2. Highlighted Column Chart

I used a highlighted column chart to show the specific brands or price ranges within the overall price distribution. This helps in drawing attention to outliers highest number data points.

This chart highlights significant variations within the data, such as particularly high or low prices for certain brand.

3. Pie Chart

A pie chart was selected to represent the market share of each brand. This type of chart is ideal for showing proportions and is visually intuitive for understanding how the market is divided among different brands.

This chart communicates the relative presence of each brand in the dataset. Larger slices indicate a greater share, suggesting which brands are more prevalent or popular in the market. It provides a quick visual impression of market dominance.

4. Scatter Plot

I chose a scatter plot to examine the relationship between computer prices and brand. This chart type is excellent for displaying correlations and patterns within the data.

This chart communicates how computer prices relate to brands. A positive trend in the data points would indicate a higher price. This helps in understanding how much consumers might need to pay for each brand.

Each of these visualizations was chosen to provide a clear and comprehensive analysis of computer prices by brand. The column chart offers a straightforward comparison of average prices, the highlighted column chart draws attention to notable variations, the pie chart reveals market share, and the scatter plot uncovers correlations between price and brand. Together, these charts provide a multifaceted view of the dataset, making it easier to derive meaningful insights about the computer market.
