Assignment 2

Visualization Makeover

    Source

Misrepresenting data: Before

The original graph titled "Microsoft Revenue Breakdown" presented misleading data visualization. The x-axis listed product/service categories such as "Server Products & Cloud Services," "Office Products & Cloud Services," "Windows," and others, while the y-axis showed dollar amounts. However, it likely plotted the revenue for each data point (e.g., Server Products & Cloud Services: $79.97B) rather than the total revenue for each category. This method is misleading for a revenue breakdown by category.

Correct visualization: After

The normal bar chart offers a clearer comparison between categories by displaying the total revenue for each. Each bar represents a category's total revenue, allowing viewers to easily compare the relative size of each segment. This method is much simpler than trying to decipher individual data points scattered along an axis, as seen in the original graph. Adhering to best practices for data visualization, the normal bar chart uses the x-axis to represent product/service categories (nominal data) and the y-axis to represent total revenue (quantitative data). This standard approach makes the data easier to understand and interpret accurately, focusing on the overall contribution of each category to Microsoft's revenue.

In conclusion, the normal bar chart provides a clear, concise, and accurate representation of the Microsoft revenue breakdown by product/service category, making it a more effective visualization compared to the original graph.
