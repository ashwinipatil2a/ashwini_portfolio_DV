Goodreads' Best Books Ever

Data Visualization Project

Overview:

Welcome to my data visualization project titled "Goodreads' Best Books Ever Through Data Visualization." In this project, I will analyze the Goodreads' Best Books Ever dataset, which contains information about highly rated books, including their authors, ratings, publication years, and genres. My goal is to turn this data into clear, interactive, and informative visualizations. I have taken this dataset from Kaggle. You can find the dataset by clicking on Source: Source

I have used Tableau for the visualization. I have created various charts, graph and Dashboard to help readers understand trends and patterns in the dataset. By visualizing this information, I aim to highlight most popular books, how book ratings have changed over time, which genres are favored by readers, and book popularity by language and genre, . This project will make it easier to explore and understand the data, providing valuable insights for book lovers and researchers alike.

Objective:

The objective of this project is to explore and visualize the Goodreads' Best Books Ever dataset. Through this project, I aim to uncover meaningful insights and trends within the dataset, such as popular genres, highly rated books, authors with the most highly ranks and awards, most liked books by language, and popularity trend over time through a Dashboard. By employing data visualization techniques, the project will provide a comprehensive and visually appealing analysis that highlights key patterns and trends. This will aid in understanding the preferences and behaviors of Goodreads users, and offer valuable information for readers, authors, and publishers.

Most liked and highly rated genres

Rating distribution by genres

The circle chart displaying the average liked percentage and average rating of books by genre reveals key insights into reader preferences. By visualizing the data in this format, it's clear which genres are most appreciated and highly rated by readers. The chart is easy to interpret, with each genre distinctly colored for quick comparison. This allows for a straightforward understanding of which genres are most favored and highly rated by readers.

Most prefered language by the authors

The stacked bar chart allows easy comparison of liked percentages across different languages (x-axis) and within each language, across genres (color separation). By using stacked bars with different colors representing genres, it efficiently visualizes which language is mostly prefered by authors.

Author with Total Books by Genres

The bubble chart represents author name, genre, and book count through the size and color of bubbles, the chart provides a visually engaging and informative way to analyze the data. The arrangement of bubbles, along with clear labeling and color coding, ensures that viewers can easily interpret and derive insights from the chart.

Summary or takeaway from this project

In this Goodreads' best books ever project, I created a comprehensive dashboard to explore book ratings, popular genres, and authors. I discovered that "Here Before Kilroy" by Su Walton stands is the highest-rated book, with other top-rated books following closely. Most book ratings fall between 3.9 and 4.2, indicates readers enjoy what they read. The average ratings were consistent for many years, and peaked significantly in 2016, then decreased in the following years. The Adult and Nature genres are the most liked and highly rated genres. Then A bar graph showed that around 80% of authors prefer writing in English, covering various genres. Then Author Carolyn Keene and Agatha Christie lead the Mystery genre, while Stephen King has second highest books in Horror genre.
